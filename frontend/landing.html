The problem

Short-form is cheap to watch.
It's brutally expensive to make.

The mainstream tools were built for someone else's laptop, someone else's bandwidth and someone else's currency. The math breaks the moment you're the one making the videos.

3.5–5.5 hrs

to take one polished 60-second video from script to export — scripting, filming, rough cut, colour grade, captions, audio mix, and three separate aspect-ratio exports.

17–27 hrs/wk

burned on production by a creator posting 5× a week — before an audience ever sees a frame.

Up to two-thirds of a full-time week — before a single view, comment or rand comes back.

Data you can't afford to burn

A 1GB bundle costs 1–3 hours of minimum-wage work in South Africa. Rival browser editors ship 20–30MB of codec libraries before they can process a single frame — your data, gone before you've started.

Hardware they never designed for

The laptop most creators actually own is a dual-core i3 with 8GB RAM — the R4,000–R8,000 shelf at every SA retailer. Software video decoding chokes on exactly that machine.

Priced in someone else's currency

Desktop pro tools run $20–$60 a month — R360–R1,100 — billed in dollars to creators earning rands. An early-stage independent creator simply can't pay that.

Three walls between you and "posted." iKlippa was built to walk through all three — at the architecture level, not as an afterthought.

The architecture

Three layers, built for your reality.

No jargon. Just the three reasons iKlippa runs where other tools can't.

01

A pro engine, compiled for your browser

A custom Rust engine, compiled to WebAssembly, handles compositing, colour and effects at near-native speed — entirely on your machine. Fast on modest hardware, and your footage never leaves your device.

02

An editor built for the hardware you have

A fully custom timeline, colour panel, audio mixer and caption editor — engineered for buttery scrubbing on weak machines, with exports that render locally. What you see is what renders. Instantly, privately.

03

iKlippa Director — your AI co-pilot

Powered by IBM Granite. Tell it in plain language — "remove the dead air", "add a caption here", "match this grade" — and it executes on your timeline in real time. A creative partner, not a content generator.

The unlock

Your browser already ships a hardware video decoder. We use it.

Instead of shipping 20–30MB of downloadable codec libraries like every other browser editor, iKlippa talks directly to WebCodecs — the hardware decoder already inside your browser. The whole app loads in under 2MB. That's not a marketing number; it's the architecture.
